Self-Imitation Learning

Junhyuk Oh, Yijie Guo, Satinder Singh, Honglak Lee

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.05635

Key Idea:

  • Try to reinforce an agent's past behaviour if it led to return that's better than what is currently expected.
    • Maintain past episodes in a replay buffer. Given a state, imitate a past action if it resulted in return that was higher than the current critic's value estimate.
    • Can be applied to any actor-critic architecture. It does not require any off-policy correction like importance weighting.
    • Indirectly improves exploration.

Self-Imitation Learning (SIL)

  • Assume a simple Advantage Actor-Critic (A2C) [Mnih et al.]1 agent. The training objective for the agent is as follows:

\[ \mathcal{L}^{a2c} =\mathbb{E}_{s, a \sim \pi_{\theta}}[\mathcal{L}_{actor}^{a2c}+\beta^{a2c} \mathcal{L}_{critic}^{a2c}] \hspace{42px} \]

\[ \mathcal{L}_{actor}^{a2c} =-\log \pi_{\theta}(a_{t} | s_{t})(V_{t}^{n}-V_{\theta}(s_{t}))-\alpha \mathcal{H}_{t}^{\pi_{\theta}} \]

\[ \mathcal{L}_{critic}^{a2c} =\frac{1}{2}\|V_{\theta}(s_{t})-V_{t}^{n}\|^{2} \hspace{80px} \]

\(\quad \quad\) where \(V_\theta^n = \sum_{d=0}^{n-1} \gamma^dr_{t+d} + \gamma^nV_\theta(s_{t+n})\) is the n-step bootstrapped value, \(\mathcal{H}_{t}^{\pi_{\theta}} \) is the entropy of the policy and \(\alpha\) is the entropy regularization coefficient.

  • After an episode \(\{(s_0,a_0,r_0),\dots,(s_T,a_T,r_T)\}\) is completed, its every transition \((s_t, a_t, R_t=\sum_{k=t}^T \gamma^{k-t}r_k) \) is added to the replay buffer \(\mathcal{D}\).
  • Perform \( M \) steps of the self-imitation learning by sampling a mini-batch of \((s, a, R)\) from \(\mathcal{D}\) and updating the actor and the critic using the following (familiar-looking) objective:

\[ \mathcal{L}^{sil} =\mathbb{E}_{s, a, R \in \mathcal{D}}[\mathcal{L}_{actor}^{sil}+\beta^{sil} \mathcal{L}_{critic}^{sil}] \]

\[\mathcal{L}_{actor}^{sil} =-\log \pi_{\theta}(a | s)(R-V_{\theta}(s))_{+} \]

\[\mathcal{L_{critic}}^{sil} =\frac{1}{2}\|(R-V_{\theta}(s))_{+}\|^{2} \hspace{20px}\]

\(\quad \quad\) where \( (.)_{+} = \text{max} (.,0) \).

  • The \( (.)_{+} \) operator ensures that the agent is only updated when the past return is greater than the current value estimate of the critic \((R>V_{\theta})\). This encourages the agent to imitate is own past good decisions when they resulted in larger returns than expected.
  • Can be used with Prioritized Experience Replay [Schaul et al.]2 using the clipped advantage \( (R - V_{\theta}(s) )_{+})\) as the priority.
  • The SIL objective \( \mathcal{L}^{sil} \) is theoretically justified as a form of lower-bound-soft-Q-Learning in an entropy-regularized RL framework.

Results

  • The paper presents extensive experiments on tasks from numerous domains.
    • A gridworld based Key-Door-Treasure domain which tested A2C and A2C+SIL, both with and without an additional count-based exploration method (EXP) that gives exploration bonus rewards. A2C+SIL outperforms A2C (+/- EXP) and A2C+SIL+EXP does even better.
    • On hard exploration ATARI games (Montezuma, Freeway, Gravitar, etc), A2C+SIL always significantly outperforms A2C which learns almost nothing on its own.
    • On Mujoco tasks (Swimmer, Hopper, HalfCheetah, etc), they use PPO instead of A2C. PPO+SIL does improve in performance over plain PPO, although not as significant as ATARI. This was attributed to the smooth, dense reward signal. To further verify this, delayed-reward versions of the environment were created which accumulates rewards over 20 steps. PPO+SIL shows a remarkable improvement in this case.
